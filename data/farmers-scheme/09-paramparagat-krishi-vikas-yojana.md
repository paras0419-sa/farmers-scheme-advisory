# Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana (PKVY)

**Ministry:** Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare
**Launch Year:** 2015
**Type:** Organic Farming Promotion
**Official Website:** https://www.myscheme.gov.in/schemes/pkvy

---

## Objective

Promote organic farming in India through a cluster-based approach, encouraging farmers to adopt organic practices to restore ecological balance, improve soil health, and enhance farmer livelihoods.

## Benefits

- Financial assistance of Rs 31,500 per hectare over 3 years
- Rs 15,000/ha provided directly to farmers through DBT for organic inputs
- Remaining funds for training, certification, and marketing support
- End-to-end support from production to processing, certification, and marketing
- Free organic certification through Participatory Guarantee System (PGS-India)

## Cluster-Based Approach

- Organic farming is promoted in clusters of 20 hectares each
- Each cluster has approximately 50 or more farmers
- Farmers within a cluster adopt organic farming collectively
- Lead Resource Persons (LRPs) guide farmers in each cluster

## Eligibility

- All farmers in India
- Maximum landholding limit: 2 hectares
- Farmers must commit to organic farming for a minimum of 3 years
- Must be part of an organized cluster (group of 50+ farmers)

## What is Covered

- Organic input production (vermicompost, bio-fertilizers, bio-pesticides)
- Training and capacity building on organic farming practices
- Organic certification (PGS-India)
- Marketing and branding support for organic produce
- Exposure visits and demonstrations

## How to Apply

1. Contact nearest District Agriculture Office or KVK
2. Form or join a farmer group/cluster in your area
3. Register through the designated portal or through State Agriculture Department
4. Get enrolled under PGS-India for organic certification

## Documents Required

- Aadhaar Card
- Land records
- Bank account details
- Willingness declaration for organic farming

## FY 2025-26 Status

- Target: 6 lakh hectares under organic farming by 2025-26
- 52,289 clusters formed
- 25.30 lakh farmers benefitted (as of February 2025)
- 15 lakh hectares currently under organic farming
