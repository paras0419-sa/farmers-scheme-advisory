# Soil Health Card Scheme (SHC)

**Ministry:** Ministry of Agriculture and Farmers Welfare
**Launch Year:** 2015
**Type:** Soil Testing and Advisory
**Official Website:** https://soilhealth.dac.gov.in/

---

## Objective

Promote balanced use of fertilizers and improve soil health by providing farmers with detailed soil analysis reports and crop-specific fertilizer recommendations, leading to improved crop productivity and reduced input costs.

## Benefits

- Free soil testing for farmers
- Detailed Soil Health Card with 12 key soil parameters
- Crop-specific fertilizer recommendations
- Updated every 2-3 years
- Helps reduce unnecessary expenditure on fertilizers
- Improves soil fertility over time through guided nutrient management

## Soil Parameters Tested (12 Parameters)

| Macro Nutrients | Micro Nutrients | Physical Properties |
|----------------|----------------|-------------------|
| Nitrogen (N) | Zinc (Zn) | pH |
| Phosphorus (P) | Iron (Fe) | Electrical Conductivity (EC) |
| Potassium (K) | Copper (Cu) | Organic Carbon (OC) |
| Sulphur (S) | Manganese (Mn) | |
| | Boron (B) | |

## Eligibility

- All farmers in India with cultivable land
- Both land-owning and tenant farmers
- No restriction on land size (small, marginal, and large farmers all eligible)
- No strict documentation requirements

## How to Apply / Get Your Card

1. Visit the Soil Health Card Portal (soilhealth.dac.gov.in)
2. Or contact nearest agriculture office, KVK (Krishi Vigyan Kendra), or soil testing lab
3. Soil samples are collected by trained personnel
4. Results and recommendations are provided as a printed card or accessible online

## How to Use the Card

1. Check soil health status for your plot
2. Follow crop-wise fertilizer recommendations
3. Apply recommended doses of nutrients
4. Get soil re-tested after 2-3 years to track improvement
5. Adjust fertilizer use based on updated recommendations

## FY 2025-26 Status

- Over 25 crore Soil Health Cards issued nationwide
- Increased funding for soil testing infrastructure
- New initiatives: mobile testing units, Lab-to-Land campaigns
- District-wise progress tracking on PM Kisan Portal
